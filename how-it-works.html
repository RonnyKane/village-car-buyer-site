The Process

3 Simple Steps to Cash in Hand

No dealerships, no driving, no lowball offers. Just a fair price, paid cash, at your home.

1

Submit Your Car's Information

Fill out our quick online form or simply give us a call at (352) 552-8806. All we need to get started is:

  • Your vehicle's year, make, and model
  • Approximate mileage
  • Your name and best callback number
  • Any known issues (we buy cars with mechanical problems too!)

That's it! One of our friendly team members will call you back quickly — often within the hour — with a preliminary offer. No pressure, no obligation.

2

We Come Directly to You

We schedule a convenient appointment and come to your home, your driveway, your office — wherever works best for you. You don't have to go anywhere.

Our licensed and insured team member will:

  • Do a quick visual inspection of the vehicle
  • Verify the VIN and vehicle history
  • Review your title and registration
  • Make you a firm, fair cash offer right on the spot

The inspection typically takes just 15–20 minutes. We respect your time.

3

Get Paid — Cash, Same Day

Once you accept the offer, we handle all the paperwork and pay you cash on the spot from Citizens First Bank of The Villages. No waiting. No holds. No bounced checks.

Most competitors place a 7-day hold on funds. We do not. You have your money the same day we pick up the car.

Be Prepared

What to Have Ready

Having these items on hand makes the process quick and smooth.

✅ Required Documents

  • 📄 Vehicle Title — signed and in your name
  • 🪪 Government-Issued Photo ID — driver's license or passport
  • 📋 Vehicle Registration — current Florida registration
  • 🔑 All Sets of Keys — any keys, fobs, remotes

ℹ️ Good to Have

  • 📁 Maintenance Records — oil changes, repairs (can increase value)
  • 🔖 Window Sticker / Monroney — if still available
  • 🏦 Lien Payoff Info — if you still owe money on the car
  • 📦 Owner's Manual — and any extra accessories
Have a lien on the car? No problem! We can still buy it. We'll work directly with your lender to pay off the remaining balance and give you the difference. See our FAQ for details.
